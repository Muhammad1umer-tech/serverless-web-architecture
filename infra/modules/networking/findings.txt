This route table code only controls outbound traffic routing — it tells AWS where to send traffic going out from your VPC (like to the internet).

Inbound traffic (coming into your VPC or instances) isn’t controlled by the route table. Instead, inbound access is managed by:

Security Groups: They act like virtual firewalls for your instances, controlling what inbound (and outbound) network traffic is allowed.

Network ACLs (Access Control Lists): They control traffic at the subnet level, both inbound and outbound.

Also, for inbound traffic from the internet to reach your instances, the instances need:

A public IP or be behind a load balancer.

Appropriate security group rules allowing inbound connections on certain ports (e.g., port 80 for HTTP).

A VPC endpoint allows your AWS resources in a private VPC (e.g., Lambda, EC2) to connect to AWS
services without going through the internet.

Gateway Endpoint	S3 and DynamoDB only
Interface Endpoint	Most other AWS services (SSM, Secrets Manager, etc.)
